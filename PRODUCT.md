# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Solo ecommerce founders and in-house marketing managers running Shopify stores with active Meta ad spend. They arrive frustrated: the ads look fine on paper but the revenue doesn't match. They're not beginners — they've already tried tweaking creative, copy, and targeting — and they want a clear diagnosis from someone who knows what they're looking at, not another generic checklist or a pitch for a retainer.

## Product Purpose

A paid audit service (£397 flat fee) that finds the gap between the ad click and the checkout conversion. The site's one job is to get the right person to book. Success is a completed Stripe payment from someone who has a Shopify store, is running Meta ads, and is ready to act on the findings.

## Brand Personality

Warm, expert, reassuring. Julia is a real person with a track record, not an agency. The voice is direct and specific — she tells you what's wrong and what to do about it — but never cold or salesy. The site should feel like a recommendation from a trusted specialist, not a hard sell.

## Anti-references

- Generic agency / SaaS blue aesthetic
- Loud, hypey marketing pages (countdown timers, fake urgency, excessive caps)
- Corporate or cold — polished but impersonal, big-company feel
- Cheap or untrustworthy — design that undermines credibility at £397 price point

## Design Principles

1. **Specific beats clever.** Every claim earns its place with a concrete detail. No buzzwords, no vague promises.
2. **Trust is built visually.** The design itself is evidence of the quality of the work. Inconsistency, sloppiness, or generic patterns undermine the sale before a word is read.
3. **One decision per page.** Everything on the site points toward one action: booking. Reduce noise, remove detours.
4. **Warmth through precision.** Reassurance comes from specificity — exact timelines, exact deliverables, exact process — not from softening language or decorative friendliness.
5. **Julia is the product.** The personality, voice, and aesthetic should feel like a person, not a brand machine.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a minimum. Reduced motion respected. No reliance on colour alone to convey meaning.
